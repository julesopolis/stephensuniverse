The works of Stephen King, one of America's most prolific and influential writers, chart a path across a fictional United States of normal towns beset by unnatural events, of everyday lives that become entangled with eldritch terrors. The real world locations (or closest analogues) of the places mapped from Stephen King’s stories are a slice of the dark America imagined within the pages of his works. From the hedonistic spires of Las Vegas where survivors of a deadly epidemic gather in preparation for a final clash of good and evil to the semi-fictional town of Derry, home of the otherworldly horror of It, each icon represents the site of a King story. These locations blend the real and the imagined, as the fictional gives way to the real and vice versa within the confines of this map.
